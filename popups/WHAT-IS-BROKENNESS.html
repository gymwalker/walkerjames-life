What is Brokenness (Continued)...

Brokenness is the emotional, mental, or spiritual pain that leaves you feeling discouraged, crushed, or hopeless. It can make life feel unbearable, relationships feel empty, and every day feels like a fight just to get through.

Brokenness steals your joy. It robs your peace. It drains your purpose.

The Many Faces of Brokenness

Brokenness doesn’t always look the same, but it often stems from:

  • Loss – A loved one, a job, a relationship, a dream, financial freedom

  • Betrayal – Lies, abandonment, or abuse from someone you trusted

  • Trauma – Emotional, physical, or sexual abuse… bullying… injustice… violence

  • Uncontrollable Circumstances – When someone else’s choices damage your life and leave you cleaning up the mess

  • Inner Struggles – Shame, regret, insecurity, unworthiness, depression, suicidal thoughts

And here’s the brutal reality: hurt people often hurt people.

Broken people break people—intentionally or not—and the cycle keeps going until someone decides to stop it.

How Brokenness Shows Up

It doesn't always look like sadness. It might look like:

  • Explosive Anger – Because it's easier than admitting you're hurt

  • Isolation – Withdrawing because it’s safer to shut down than risk being rejected

  • Addictions – Alcohol, porn, drugs, social media, food… anything to escape the pain

  • Self-Punishment – Cutting, sabotaging relationships, believing you deserve the pain

  • Head Noise – The non-stop inner critic saying, “You’ll never be enough. You’re a failure. No one really cares.”

Sound familiar?

It should—because brokenness doesn’t discriminate.

It doesn’t care how old you are, how much money you make, what church you go to, or how “put together” you look. At some point, every one of us wrestles with it—either directly or through the people we love.

Left unhealed, brokenness creates pain that lasts for years… and sometimes for generations.
