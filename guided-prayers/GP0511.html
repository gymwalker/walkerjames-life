A Prayer of Gratitude for Being Made New

God, thank You for not leaving me where You found me. Thank You for stepping into my brokenness, my rebellion, my mess — and loving me through it. Thank You for the cross that didn’t just forgive my sin, but made a way for me to become someone new. I’m not who I used to be — and that’s only because of You.

You didn’t just clean me up; You gave me a new heart. A new spirit. A new name. A new future. You called me out of darkness and into light. You exchanged my shame for honor, my guilt for grace, my fear for peace. You’ve redefined my identity — not based on my past, but based on Your love. And for that, I’m overwhelmed with gratitude.

Help me live like someone who’s been made new. Help me to walk in the freedom You’ve given me — not going back to old chains, not living under old lies. Remind me daily that I am a new creation. That the old has gone, and the new has come. Fill me with joy every time I remember where You brought me from. And let that joy overflow into praise.

God, I want to honor You with this new life. I want to use it to serve, to love, to bless, to shine Your light and Your love everywhere I go. I want people to see me and know that something is different — not because I’m better, but because I’ve been redeemed. Let my story be a testimony of Your mercy. Let my life point to Your grace.

I want what You want — not just a fresh start, but a strong, close, deep, loving, personal, passionate, intimate relationship with You. One where I live each day in gratitude for the transformation You’ve done in me. One where being made new is not just a moment, but a lifestyle of walking with You.

Thank You for making all things new — especially me.

In Jesus’ name, Amen.
